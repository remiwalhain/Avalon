Avalon is a game of hidden loyalty.

Players are either Loyal Servants of Arthur or aligned with the Evil ways of Mordred.

Good wins the game by successfully completing three Quests.

Evil wins if three Quests end in failure.

Evil can also win by assassinating Merlin at game's end or if a Quest cannot be undertaken.

Players may make any claims during the game, at any point in the game. Discussion, deception, accusation, and logical deduction are all equally important in order for Good to prevail or Evil to rule the day.

The game consists of several Rounds; each Round has a Team Building phase and a Quest phase. In the Team Building phase the Leader proposes a Team to complete a Quest - all the players will either approve the proposed Team and move to the Quest phase, or reject the proposed Team passing leadership to the next player and repeating the process until a Team is approved. In the Quest phase those players selected to be on the Team will determine if the Quest is successful.

Choose your Team wisely. Approve only Teams where you trust everyone. Even a single Evil player on the team is enough for failure. Roles are assigned randomly after the start of the game and should not be revealed to other players.

Roles

Loyal Servants of Arthur are on the side of Good.

Minions of Mordred are on the side of Evil and know each other's identity.

Merlin knows the identity of the Evil players.

Assassin can win the game by assassinating Merlin in case three Quests succeed.

Optional Extra Roles

Percival knows Merlin's identity.

Mordred's identity is not revealed to Merlin.

Oberon does not reveal himself to Evil players.

Morgana appears as Merlin to Percival.
